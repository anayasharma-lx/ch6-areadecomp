Area Decomposition

Multiplication is area. A rectangle with sides (a + m) and (b + n) splits naturally into four smaller rectangles — and the four areas add up to the whole. That's Identity 1. Try changing the sign of m or n: the negative piece becomes a cutout instead of an addition.

The four identities at a glance
Sign convention: signs propagate through every term
(a + m)(b + n) = ab + an + mb + mn
(a + m)(b − n) = ab − an + mb − mn
(a − m)(b + n) = ab + an − mb − mn
(a − m)(b − n) = ab − an − mb + mn

All four follow one rule: every term of the left bracket multiplies every term of the right bracket. The sign of each product follows the usual integer rules. The picture changes — some pieces become cutouts — but the algebra is the same identity, just with signs.
